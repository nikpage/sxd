The Way

Design always means seeking an original solution. That means venturing into the unknown, it means risk. That's the only way to innovate, to invent a new solution.

We are guided by core principles we've learned in our practices. Simplicity is chief among them and often the hardest to achieve.

Since every design effort is unique we don't have checklists or repeatable steps but rather a collection of ingredients some of which may be appropriate for a given project and others for another.  That collection is ever evolving along with our external business environment but includes:

 

Goal or Purpose

Target Audience

Target's Reason to Care

A goal is what separates design from art. Understanding yours is key to successful design. Everything in the design must support this goal. Everything.Anything "designed for everyone" is probably going to fail. Defining a target group lets us understand their needs that we can design specifically to meet.The harsh reality of life is that no one gives a shit about your goals. We need to design the reason for the target group to be interested, to care at all.

Clarity

Legend

Engagement

Project teams, especially in startups are often way too smart. The result is an overly complex idea. Distilling it till we find the essential elements can be important.More than a story, a legend introduces values, archetypes, things to believe in and want to be part of. Elements of the design should give satisfaction and that leads to a desire to take more action. This builds engagement not only within the design but outside it such as telling people about it.
